Personal Statement

Despite playing Video Games for as long as I can remember, I had never entertained the idea of working in the industry until recently. During my Physics degree and my work I discovered that I am most motivated when I am able to use my skills to create a tangible product or experience, whether that be projecting the yield of a nuclear blast or a meticulously crafted drink. Throughout my time studying at university I have also worked in multiple customer facing roles which, not only has taught me the importance of time management but also the importance of working effectively in a team to ensure service runs smoothly and efficiently. I believe this experience will come to bolster my effectiveness as a programmer, working as part of a larger team where many different roles come together to work on a product.

Education

Game Engineering- Newcastle University MSc
Newcastle University | Sep 2022 - Sep 2023: I am on track to achieve a distinction in this MSc

  • Advanced Programming for Games: This module served as an introduction to C++ and coding by introducing all of the core aspects of C++ that would act as a backbone for everything to come. During this module I made my first program.
  • Advanced Graphics for Games: Using OpenGL I was tasked with creating a fantasy scene. I found my previous study of Physics, particularly my proficiency in 3D math, valuable as this is an integral part of vertex manipulation.
  • Advanced Game Technologies: In this module I worked to make a physics engine. Having come from a Physics background I found this extremely enjoyable to apply some of the skills I had learnt from my undergraduate degree in a practical way.
  • Engineering Gaming Solutions within a Team: Currently working on a team project to make a Splatoon-like game. I am in the project focusing on graphics and cross-platform compatibility with PS4.
  • Dissertation: Using my physics background I implemented Smooth Particle Hydrodynamics into the Newcastle University game engine, solving all fluid particle calculations in compute shaders and providing the ability for fluid bodies to act on rigid bodies. The simulation currently supports up to 7200 particles being rendered with forces being updated on the GPU achieving 240 frames per second in most scenarios.

3D

Grade- Distinction

Using C++, OpenGL and PS4 SDK

During the second semester I took part in a team project to recreate the game SplatToon, by the end of this project my team became the 2nd team in the courses' history to create a fully working game in all aspects that were assigned by the professors, and the first team to do so by making a 3D game. My role particularly in this project was to be responsible for integrating a Playstation 4 version of the game. Using the Playstion 4 SDK I rewrote the renderer to help allow the game to run on Playstion 4 as well as using cMAKE to get all other parts of the game to work cross-platform. Throughout this project there was a focus especially on ensuring the scope and objectives we had set were achievable within the limited timeframe.
